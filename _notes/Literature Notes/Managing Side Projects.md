# Managing Side Projects
Created: 2022-06-06 11:50
Status: #status/litnotes 
Tags: #tags/programming 


As a developer you can ask ourselve if having S.P is important ? 
In fact, S.P are important for some reasons. 

If you are beginner, your S.P are conversations starters, they allow a convo to be natural and, most importantly, they can differentiate you from the rest of the candidates. 
Don't force time in your S.P, it's only with a good organization that the time will come for it. 

The first thing to do is to start by organize what you spend your time on (FT work, activities, relaxing, etc). Create a schedul that allow you to find some time for your S.P.

Before each session on your S.P, you need to spend 10 minutes thinking of what youre gonna do, what feature you want implement, what bug you want to disapear. 
One each simply way to do that, is writing things down on a piece of paper and writing things done, one by one. 
Even with the best schedule, sometimes scrifice is needed for you S.P, and it's fine. 

A lot of us are busy, lot of us already have FT jobs, some of us have families, we all have different days but if you really want to find time, you'll find and you'll face some benefit.
It's a awesome learning way with a lot of fun. You can solve your own problems and setting personal goals for your S.P. 

Another big question about S.P, is : how much learning should I incorporate into my S.P ? 
If your S.P is a serious investment on your part, like building a S.A.A.S, learning new tech may not be appropriate beacause in this situation, you are more focused on delivering and creating usable code using your knowledge in the best possible way.

In the other hand if youre working on something for yourself, it's more cool and you can definitely incorporate a lot of learning. You want to learn Redux, incorporate it on your S.P ! You want to learn TypeScript, incorporate it on your S.P ! 
It's a very good way to force yourself to learn a new technology fully by yourself and really learning how to use it.
To lean, you need to get your hands dirty !

You have to be careful to the Shiny Dollar Syndrome.
Imagine youre walking down the street. As youre walking, you see a shiny dollar on the ground. You get super excited, so you walk towards it to pick it up. But, before you do, in the corner of you eyes, you see another shinier dollar. So you stop, and then you walk towards the SHINNIER dollar, to pick up. 
This is what happens with S.P, you think of an idea, you get excited, and you start working on it. But then, you get ANOTHER great S.P, then you start working on that one.. repeats.. and repeats..
This syndrome is also valid for learning new langage as JR/beginer.

To prevent Shiny Dollar Syndrome you can practice some tricks. First of all, it's important to clarify one things : finishing a task is a skill in itself. 
Develop habit of finishing things, and always keep your focus on the first goal. Why did you start this S.P ? If you have a good reason, you'll stay motivated and focused. 

Sometimes, it's just your brain tricks you and get bored. Get into the habit of setting goals, and checkpoint for your SP. You can also set an expiration date's S.P.
You can loose the love for your current S.P, if you set an expiration date a month ahead, in a months time, check in yourself. See if you still like working ont it, see if you have been working on it. If you're passionate about it, renew the expiration date. Proceed in this way will help you take a step back on your S.P and take decision. 

And if you decide to stop your S.P, it's ok no worries. You just have to be clear with which projects is in progress and witch are cancelled.

You already have a F.T job, I think you also need to have S.P. Even if you're already hired, you need to never stop learning, never stop trying.
