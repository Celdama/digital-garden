# Zettelkasten Method With Obsidian
Created: 2022-06-06 09:33
Status: #status/litnotes
Tags: #tags/PKM

The Zettelkasten Methods isn't easy to learn, because there are not many resources available online. I think, this is a very good way to store all our consume's content. 

This method contains 4 principles to be very useful:
- writing is the only thing that matters : learn and practice writing is an essential skills on our personal life. 
- simplicity : at the start, this system can be a little messy, but becomes more and more valuable as it grows.
- nobody starts from scratch : this system will can be helps you to doesn't start from scratch when you want to write a blog post etc
- let the work carry you forward : 


Obsidian is a free and open-source software, using markdown formats for your notes. You can use this awesome tools just like a simple note-taking tool or you can also manage your entire life, it's up to you. 


In this system, you'll use 3 differents type's note

**fleeting notes** : it's simply a basic notes, reminders of what you have on your head, only reminders. They can be written in any kind of way, on your phone, on paper etc...

**literature notes** : in literature notes, you'll use your fleeting notes and develop your idea with your own words. You can also use directly this type of notes when you read a book, make notes about the content. 
The things to keep in mind is, you have to keep very selective on what you want to select from your book and the most important is, use your own words. 
You have to understand first and write the idea in your own words. 
Keep bibliographical details about your content. 
The main purpose of this type's note is to help with writing permanent notes. 

**permanent notes** : the idea here is not to collect but to develop ideas, arguments and discussions. You have to use full sentences in your permanent notes, make references between all your notes and be as precise, clear, and brief as possible.
This notes will never be thrown away and contain the necessary information in themselves in a permanently understanble way. In brief, it is theses notes who's add actual value to your system. 
You have to keep this notes atomic, one note can only have one idea and you make references between notes when it's relevant.
